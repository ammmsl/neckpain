## 1. Baseline Assessment (Day 1)

Complete these assessments before starting the protocol. Film each test and save with date. These are your comparison points for the 3-week reassessment.

### 1.1 Primary Metric — Leahy Test

This is your single most important tracking metric. It integrates shoulder flexion, external rotation, lat length, pec length, and scapulohumeral rhythm into one measurement.

**Protocol**

Sit upright with a high chest, looking forward. Hold the centre of a stick in a supinated (palms-up) grip. With the arm locked, slowly lift the stick vertically. The stick must remain parallel to the floor (no twisting). The elbow must remain straight. If the elbow bends or the stick twists, you have exceeded your range — reset and record the angle before that point.

**Recording**

Place a camera on the floor angled slightly upward for repeatable measurement. Record the maximum angle without elbow bending or stick twisting. Test each arm separately. Record both left and right angles.

> *Expected baseline: Left side will show a deficit vs right on the Leahy Test. The left-right difference is your asymmetry marker.*

### 1.2 Component Tests

If you do not achieve 180° on the Leahy Test (which is expected given the current presentation), run through these to identify which structures are the primary limiters:

| Test                    | Protocol                                                                                                               | Target                            | Implication if Failed                                                |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Active vs Passive**   | Compare supine (passive), wall (perpendicular), bent-over (active) shoulder flexion. Maintain firm lower-back contact. | Active within 10° of passive      | Large gap → prioritise active-based training over passive stretching |
| **Lat Length**          | Supine, knees to chest (spine flexed) — lower arms overhead. Compare to hook-lying (spine flat).                       | No angle change between positions | Angle changes → lats restricting overhead range                      |
| **External Rotation**   | Sit with elbow on knee, upper arm in line with shoulder. Externally rotate forearm.                                    | 45° past vertical (min: 5–10°)    | Below minimum → high priority ER stretching (subscap/teres major)    |
| **Pec Major (partner)** | Sit upright, thumbs back, arms relaxed. Partner lifts wrists to ear height and pulls both arms back.                   | 45° past shoulder line            | Below target → pec stretch required (expected to fail on left)       |
| **Pec Minor**           | Supine, arms slightly out. Partner presses shoulders toward floor.                                                     | No stretch felt                   | Stretch felt → pec minor tight (expected positive on left)           |

> *Given the clinical findings, expect failures on pec major, pec minor, and likely lat length and ER on the left. This will confirm the physio assessment and validate the exercise selection below.*

## 2. Weekly Schedule Overview

This protocol runs alongside your existing gymnastics (3x/week) and gym (4x/week) training. It is designed to slot in without adding separate sessions — the anterior release work is daily habit-level volume, the strengthening integrates into gym days, and the band work can serve as warm-up.

| Component | Frequency | Duration | When |
|-----------|-----------|----------|------|
| **Anterior Release + ER** | Daily, ideally 2x/day (AM + PM) | 12–18 min per session | Morning: upon waking. Evening: before bed or post-training. |
| **Posterior Strengthening** | 4x/week (on gym days) | 12–15 min | Integrate into gym warm-up or accessory block. |
| **Band Mobility** | Daily (can combine with anterior release) | 5–8 min | Warm-up before training or standalone on rest days. |
| **Postural Interruption** | Workdays (Sun–Thu + Sat) | 10 seconds every 30 minutes | Set phone timer during desk work. |

### Training Modifications (Active for Entire 3-Week Block)

**Front Squat:** Switch to cross-arm grip to change bar position relative to AC joint. Monitor for AC joint swelling — if present, reduce load.

**Overhead Pressing:** Reduce volume and load. No heavy overhead work until Leahy Test shows meaningful improvement. Bodyweight overhead (handstands) is acceptable if pain-free.

**Gymnastics:** Continue. Use awareness of cervical extension when tracking high throws in Ultimate Frisbee. Continue general mobility work below the neck.

**Sleeping:** Avoid sofa sleeping. If side-sleeping, alternate sides or use a pillow between the arm and torso to prevent sustained shoulder protraction.

## 3. Anterior Release Programme (Daily)

The goal is sustained tissue lengthening — physically adding sarcomeres back to muscles that have adaptively shortened. This requires long holds (60–90 seconds minimum), not 15-second stretches. Think of this as structural remodelling, not warm-up stretching.

> *Unilateral rule: Always start with the left (weaker) side first. Match reps and depth on the right — do not go deeper on the right during this correction phase.*

### 3.1 Pec Stretch — Doorframe (Contract-Relax)

**Targets: Pectoralis Major + Pectoralis Minor**

**Setup:** Stand in a doorframe. Place the forearm and elbow against the frame. Experiment with hand height — higher targets the sternal fibres, lower targets the clavicular fibres. Drive the shoulder forward to engage across the pec and into the bicep.

| Parameter | Prescription |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Method 1: Passive** | Hold 90 seconds. On each out-breath, push slightly deeper into the stretch. |
| **Method 2: Contract-Relax** | Push into the wall at near-maximal effort for 5 seconds (contract), then relax for 5 seconds and deepen. Repeat for 6 cycles (60s total). |
| **Method 3: Contract & Lift** | Push into the wall for 5 seconds, then attempt to lift the hand away from the wall while maintaining position. This maps new end-range actively. |
| **Sets** | 2–3 per side |
| **Rest** | 90 seconds between sets |
| **Variation** | Bent-Arm: Bending the arm removes the biceps and forearms from the equation, isolating the pecs more directly. Alternate between straight-arm and bent-arm across sessions. |

> *Week 1: Use passive holds to establish baseline positions. Week 2–3: Progress to contract-relax and contract-and-lift methods for faster tissue change.*

### 3.2 Long Head of Bicep Stretch

**Target: Long Head of Biceps Brachii**

**Setup:** Arm behind the body, palm facing up (supinated). Extend at both the elbow and shoulder. Use a doorframe or wall behind you for leverage.

**Hold:** 60–90 seconds per side, 2 sets. Deepen on each exhale.

> *This addresses the anterior and superior pull on the humeral head that contributes to the overhead range deficit. Often overlooked in shoulder rehab.*

### 3.3 Lat Stretch (if Lat Length Test positive)

**Target: Latissimus Dorsi**

**Standing setup:** Stand beside a bar or power cage edge. Outside arm crosses in front at head height, inside arm grabs bar at waist height (supinated grip). Sink hips down and out to the side, away from the bar. Allow the shoulder blade to elevate toward the ear. Begin to twist and look through under the arm. Push the lower arm against the bar to deepen.

| Parameter | Prescription |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Passive Hold** | 90 seconds per side, 2–3 sets |
| **Active Variation** | 10 reps: contract (retract scapula) for 3 seconds, relax and deepen for 3 seconds. 2–3 sets. |
| **Rest** | 90 seconds |

## 4. Posterior Strengthening (4x/Week)

These exercises rebuild the posterior force couple — lower trap, middle trap, rhomboids, serratus anterior — that has been chronically lengthened and weakened by the anterior shortening pattern. Without this component, released anterior structures will simply re-shorten because there is nothing holding the scapula in its corrected position.

> *Order of execution follows the toolkit's program design hierarchy: stretch first (Section 3), then strengthen (this section). On gym days, do the anterior release during warm-up, then this block as accessory work.*

### 4.1 External Rotation Stretching — Band Contract-Relax (A1)

**Targets: Subscapularis, Teres Major**

**Why included:** Baseline data confirms both sides are in deficit (left -11°, right -9° — neither reaching vertical, well below the 5–10° minimum). This is a bilateral restriction requiring daily work on both sides equally, not just left-side catch-up. Elevated from gym-day-only to daily based on severity. The ER contract-relax method can be used before a strength session without impeding performance.

| Step | Execution |
|------|-----------|
| **Setup** | Elbow-on-knee position with band pulling into external rotation. |
| **The Fight (8s)** | Contract against the band by pulling into internal rotation at near-maximal effort. |
| **Relax/Deepen** | Relax for a few seconds, breathe deeply, let the band pull deeper into ER. Shuffle slightly away from anchor. |
| **Repeat** | 4 cycles of fight → deepen. |
| **End-Range Map (15s)** | Release band. Maximally externally rotate using only your own strength for 15 seconds to map the new range. |
| **Sets / Rest** | 2–3 sets per side. 90 seconds rest. Left side first. |

### 4.2 Elbow on Knee ER Raises — 135° (B1)

**Target: External Rotators of the Rotator Cuff**

**Setup:** Sit on the floor with one leg flat and the other bent. Place the elbow on the vastus medialis (not directly on the knee). Elbow height approximately 2 inches below the shoulder. Use the 135° position (angle between upper arm and shoulder line).

**Execution:** Start with forearm as close to vertical as range allows. Lower with control to deepest achievable range without the shoulder rolling forward. Lift back up.

| Parameter | Prescription |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Reps** | 8–12 |
| **Sets** | 2–3 |
| **Tempo** | 3021 (3s lower, no pause bottom, 2s lift, 1s top) |
| **Rest** | 45 seconds |
| **Loading** | Start light. Use micro-plates (0.5 kg) to bridge gaps. Left side first, match reps on right. |

### 4.3 Trap-3 Raises (B2)

**Target: Lower Trapezius**

**Why critical:** The lower trap is the primary muscle responsible for depressing and posteriorly tilting the scapula — directly opposing the pull of the shortened pec minor. This is the single most important strengthening exercise in this protocol.

**Setup:** Adjustable bench at approximately 60°. Retract and depress the scapula (pull it down and back) before initiating the lift.

**Execution:** While maintaining retraction and depression, lift the arm overhead and up. Reset the scapular position at the bottom of every rep. The arm must at least cover the ear at the top.

| Parameter | Prescription |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Reps** | 8–12 |
| **Sets** | 2–3 |
| **Tempo** | 3120 (3s lower, 1s pause bottom, 2s lift, no pause top) |
| **Rest** | 45 seconds |
| **Active Flex (from Wk 1)** | 6-second hold at the top of each rep to develop active end-range strength. Baseline active-passive gap >10° bilaterally — this is a day-one priority, not a progression. |
| **Loading** | Use micro-plates (0.25 kg) for incremental progression. Left side first. |

### 4.4 Powell Raises (B3)

**Targets: Rhomboids, Mid-Trapezius, Rear Deltoids**

**Setup:** Lie on side with hips stacked on an adjustable bench. Knees bent forward for stability. Support head with non-working arm.

**Execution:** Maintain a constant 5° elbow bend. Initiate with scapular retraction. Lift dumbbell in a smooth arc — raised just in front of the face — until vertically above. Allow full scapular protraction at the bottom of each rep.

| Parameter | Prescription |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Reps** | 8–12 |
| **Sets** | 2–3 |
| **Tempo** | 3120 (match Trap-3 tempo) |
| **Rest** | 45 seconds |
| **Loading** | Use micro-plates (0.5 kg). Left side first. |

### 4.5 Serratus Anterior Push-Up Plus

**Target: Serratus Anterior**

**Why included:** The serratus anterior upwardly rotates and protracts the scapula — essential for the scapula to move normally during overhead reach. When it is weak, the upper trap compensates.

**Execution:** Standard push-up position. At the top of the push-up, push further — protract the scapulae as far as possible (the "plus"). Hold the protracted position for 2–3 seconds. Lower and repeat. 3 sets of 10–15 reps, 60 seconds rest.

## 5. Straps Grip Band Routine (Daily)

This is the highest-yield daily maintenance tool from the mobility toolkit. Using the aerial straps grip (band looped behind the wrist between thumb and forefinger), the shoulder can be targeted in 11+ directions with no grip tension — allowing the joints to open more deeply than bar-based drills. Can be performed as warm-up, cool-down, or standalone.

### Priority Directions for This Protocol

Focus on these 6 directions that directly target the anterior shortening pattern and posterior weakness:

| #   | Direction                            | What It Targets                                                             | Method                                                           |
| --- | ------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **Horizontal Abduction (Pec)**       | Pec major/minor stretch — the primary tissue restriction.                   | Passive: 60s hold. Active: fight the band by retracting scapula. |
| 2   | **Shoulder Flexion, Straight Arm**   | Lats and anterior capsule — overhead range.                                 | Passive hold, then active lift-offs.                             |
| 3   | **Flexion + Side Bend (Lat)**        | Deeper lat stretch combining two vectors.                                   | Passive hold: 60s per side.                                      |
| 4   | **External Rotation**                | Subscapularis and teres major — internal rotator release.                   | Contract-relax: 8s fight, deepen, repeat.                        |
| 5   | **Scapula Retraction**               | Posterior chain activation — trains the scapula to hold corrected position. | Active: fight band by retracting and depressing.                 |
| 6   | **Shoulder Extension, Straight Arm** | Anterior deltoid and bicep — complements the bicep stretch.                 | Passive hold: 60s.                                               |

Flow through these 6 directions in order, spending 60–90 seconds per direction. Left side first. Total time: 6–8 minutes. On days with more time, add the remaining 5 directions for a full exploratory session.

## 6. Postural Interruption Protocol (Workdays)

The anterior shortening pattern is driven by sustained desk posture. Even perfect stretching and strengthening will be undermined if the tissue spends 8+ hours per day in the shortened position. This protocol breaks that cycle.

### 6.1 The 30-Minute Timer

**Action:** Set a recurring timer on your phone for every 30 minutes during desk work (Sun–Thu + Sat).

**On each alarm:** 10 seconds of full scapular retraction and depression — pull the shoulder blades down and together as hard as possible. Hold. Release. Resume work.

### 6.2 Workstation Setup

**Keyboard/mouse position:** Adjust so elbows are at your sides rather than reaching forward. This reduces sustained pec minor and pec major shortening during the workday.

**Monitor height:** Top of screen at eye level to reduce cervical flexion load (relevant to the C5–C7 disc bulges).

## 7. Week-by-Week Progression

### Week 1 — Establish Baseline & Habits

| Focus | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Anterior Release** | Passive holds only (90s). Establish positions and baseline depth. Do not force depth. Left side first. |
| **Posterior Strength** | 2 sets per exercise. Find correct starting weights (err lighter). Focus on scapular position cues. Include 6-second hold at top of Trap-3 raises from day one (active-passive gap >10° on both sides — active end-range work is a priority, not a Week 2 progression). |
| **Band Work** | Passive approach — let the band pull. Get comfortable with the straps grip setup. |
| **Habit** | Install the 30-minute desk timer. Adjust workstation. Stop sofa sleeping. |
| **Training Mods** | Switch to cross-arm front squat grip. Reduce overhead pressing volume by 50%. |

### Week 2 — Intensify

| Focus | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Anterior Release** | Progress to contract-relax method on pec stretch (5s contract / 5s relax, 6 cycles). Add contract-and-lift on final set. |
| **Posterior Strength** | Increase to 3 sets. Introduce Trap-1 raises (elevation + protraction scapular position) as a second variation alongside Trap-3 — this trains the active press position that the active-passive gap indicates is weak. |
| **Band Work** | Shift to active approach — fight the band by retracting and depressing scapula. Add contract-relax on ER direction. |
| **Check** | Quick Leahy Test recheck (no formal filming needed — just note if range is improving). |

### Week 3 — Consolidate + Prepare for Reassessment

| Focus | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Anterior Release** | Continue contract-relax. If depth has improved noticeably, increase hold time to 120s on passive sets. Experiment with bent-arm pec variation to isolate pecs from biceps. |
| **Posterior Strength** | Attempt micro-plate progression (0.25 kg on Trap-3/Trap-1, 0.5 kg on ER and Powell). Maintain 3 sets. |
| **Band Work** | Full 11-direction flow on at least 2 sessions this week for comprehensive assessment of remaining restrictions. |
| **Reassessment** | Final 2 days of Week 3: Repeat full baseline assessment (Section 1). Film Leahy Test with same camera angle. Run all 5 component tests. Compare angles. Include bent-over active test with and without 2kg to track active end-range strength. |

## 9. Progress Tracking

Track the minimum needed to make data-driven decisions. Overly granular tracking adds friction that reduces compliance — and compliance is the single biggest variable in this 3-week block.

### 9.1 What to Track

| Metric | Method | Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Leahy Test angle (L/R)** | Video from floor, same angle. Use protractor app on screenshot. | Day 1, end of Week 2 (informal), end of Week 3 (formal) |
| **Pec stretch depth** | Doorframe hand position at end-range. Mark with tape. | Weekly photo |
| **ER angle (L/R)** | Video the elbow-on-knee position at max ER. | Day 1 and Day 21 |
| **Trap-3 / Powell load** | Training journal entry (weight × reps × sets). | Every session |
| **AC joint swelling** | Visual check post front-squat. Note presence/absence. | Every front squat session |
| **Compliance** | Simple checkbox: did you do the AM stretch? PM stretch? Posterior strength? Timer? | Daily |

### 9.2 Reassessment Protocol (End of Week 3)

Repeat the full baseline assessment from Section 1 using the same camera angle and conditions. Compare:

**1.** Leahy Test angle — has the left-right gap narrowed?

**2.** Leahy Test angle — has each side improved? Has the left-right gap narrowed? (This is the primary empirical metric; the physio's observational overhead gap estimate is not repeatable.)

**3.** Component tests — which structures have improved, which are still limiting?

**4.** AC joint swelling — has it reduced or disappeared during front squats?

**5.** Cervical ROM — has the residual flexion restriction at the cervicothoracic junction improved? (Downstream indicator of shoulder girdle normalisation.)

## 10. Post-Reassessment Decision Tree

After the 3-week block, use these outcomes to determine next steps:

| Outcome | Interpretation | Next Step |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Leahy angle improved >10°, gap narrowing | Anterior release is working. Structural hypothesis confirmed as primary driver. | Continue Phase 1 for another 3–6 weeks. Begin reintroducing standard front squat grip. Progress to Phase 2 exercises. |
| Leahy angle improved 5–10° | Moving in the right direction but tissue change is slow. Expected for chronic shortening. | Continue Phase 1. Increase to 2x daily contract-relax. Consider adding ESWT to accelerate anterior tissue change. |
| Leahy angle unchanged (<5°) | Either compliance was insufficient, or the structural restriction is more entrenched. Check compliance log first. | If compliance was high: escalate to physio-guided manual release + this protocol. If compliance was low: simplify and recommit. |
| Strength gains on Trap-3/Powell but no ROM change | Posterior muscles are responding but anterior tissue hasn't released yet. This is common — strength gains precede flexibility gains. | Maintain. The strength foundation is building. Double down on anterior release volume. |

> *Parallel track: The Neurobion Forte rechallenge and blood work are running concurrently. If the rechallenge shows positive results (Layer 1 improvement), this will compound with the structural correction. Neither track should wait for the other — they address different layers of the model.*
